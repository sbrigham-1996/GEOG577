Project Plan on Deforestation

1. Objective/Question/Hypothesis

How has deforestation in the Amazon Rainforest impacted the regional and global climate system?

The increasing rate of deforestation occurring in the Amazon Rainforest has a direct impact on the regional level by increasing temperature, reducing evapotranspiration, and decreasing precipitation up to 100 km from deforested areas (Staal et al., 2024). This disruption of the forest-atmosphere connection has further implications on a global scale due to the Amazon's role as a carbon sink. As deforestation increases, the Amazon may transition from a carbon sink to a carbon source, releasing CO2 instead of absorbing it. This could further exacerbate global climate change. This project aims to analyze the regional climate impacts of deforestation in Pará and explore potential global implications using land cover change and carbon flux data.

2. Area of Interest and Time Frame

Country: Brazil
State: Pará

Pará is situated in the "arc of deforestation," one of the most heavily deforested areas globally. The pattern often begins with logging, followed by agricultural use, cattle ranching, and infrastructure expansion. I will use LULC maps to assess broad patterns and then narrow to training areas for local-scale analysis. This will be combined with climate data to assess impacts on the regional and global climate systems.

Time-frame: 2000 to 2025
This 25-year span captures deforestation booms (2000–2005), policy-driven declines (2005–2012), and a resurgence of deforestation (2012–2025). This timeline will highlight key shifts in land cover and climate patterns.

3. Data and Resolutions

Spatial Resolution:

  • Landsat 5 & 8 (30m) for historical deforestation trends.
  • MODIS Terra/Aqua (250–500m) for regional trends and climate data (e.g., LST, GPP).

Temporal Resolution:

  • Landsat (16-day revisit), MODIS (daily).

Spectral Resolution:

  • Visible, NIR, SWIR bands for NDVI and land cover indices (Tasseled Cap with Landsat 5).
  • Visible, NIR, SWIR, and TIR bands for NDVI, fire detection/scarring, and climate trends.

4. Ancillary Data

  • Base Maps: High-res maps from MapBiomas for land cover validation.
  • Climate Data: ERA5 for temperature, precipitation, and evapotranspiration.

5. Data Availability

Multi-temporal datasets from Landsat 5 & 8 and MODIS ensure consistent access to monitor land cover changes and regional climate impacts. ERA5 will complement with climate data at a broader scale.

6. Evaluation of Data Match and Adjustments

The spatial and temporal resolutions are well-aligned with my objectives. Ancillary datasets will provide additional validation to ensure a robust analysis of deforestation and climate effects.
